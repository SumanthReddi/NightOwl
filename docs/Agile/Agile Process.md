## Agile Process (Scrum Ceremonies)

### 🔻 Sprint Planning

* Conducted at the **start of the sprint**.
* Team:
  * Reviews backlog items.
  * Estimates effort.
  * Commits stories based on capacity.
* During this meeting, the team defines the sprint goal and selects backlog items that align with it.

---

### 🔻 Daily Scrum / Stand-up

* **15-minute daily meeting**.
* Each member answers:
  * What did I do yesterday?
  * What will I do today?
  * Any blockers?
* This meeting helps keep the team aligned and quickly identifies any obstacles that need addressing.

---

### 🔻 Sprint Review / Demo

* Conducted at the **end of the sprint**.
* Team demonstrates completed features to stakeholders.
* Feedback is captured for future sprints.
* This ceremony provides an opportunity for stakeholders to see progress and offer input that could influence upcoming work.

---

### 🔻 Sprint Retrospective

* Conducted after sprint review.
* Focuses on **process improvement**.
* Discusses:
  * What went well?
  * What didn’t go well?
  * What can be improved?
* Retrospectives foster a culture of continuous improvement, encouraging the team to reflect and adapt their practices.

---

## Advantages of Agile Methodology

✅ Delivers working software frequently  
✅ Early detection of defects and risks  
✅ Flexible to changing requirements  
✅ Strong collaboration across teams  
✅ Continuous improvement through retrospectives

## Agile vs Waterfall (Quick Comparison)

| Agile              | Waterfall              |
| ------------------ | ---------------------- |
| Iterative          | Sequential             |
| Flexible           | Rigid                  |
| Early feedback     | Feedback at end        |
| Continuous testing | Testing at final stage |

## Additional Notes

### 🌟 Agile Principles

Agile methodology is guided by the **Agile Manifesto**, which emphasizes:

1. Individuals and interactions over processes and tools.
2. Working software over comprehensive documentation.
3. Customer collaboration over contract negotiation.
4. Responding to change over following a plan.

These principles encourage teams to focus on delivering value quickly and adapting to changes effectively.

### 🌟 Agile Frameworks

While **Scrum** is one of the most popular Agile frameworks, others include:

- **Kanban**: Focuses on visualizing work, limiting work in progress, and optimizing flow.
- **Lean**: Emphasizes eliminating waste and maximizing value.
- **Extreme Programming (XP)**: Focuses on engineering practices like pair programming, test-driven development, and continuous integration.

Each framework has its strengths and is chosen based on the specific needs of the project and team.

### 🌟 Scaling Agile

For larger organizations or projects, scaling Agile becomes necessary. Frameworks like **SAFe (Scaled Agile Framework)**, **LeSS (Large Scale Scrum)**, and **Nexus** help coordinate multiple Agile teams working towards a common goal.

By incorporating these additional notes, the documentation provides a more comprehensive view of Agile methodology, covering not just the basics but also advanced topics and variations that might be relevant for different contexts.
