# Agile Methodology

Agile is an **iterative and incremental software development approach** where requirements and solutions evolve through collaboration between cross-functional teams. Instead of delivering everything at the end, Agile delivers **working software in small, frequent increments**.

---

## Key Agile Terms

### 🏃 Sprint

* A **time-boxed iteration** where development and testing happen.
* Usually **1–4 weeks** (most teams use **2 weeks**).
* Each sprint should deliver a **potentially shippable product increment**.
* Sprints are designed to ensure consistent delivery of value, with each sprint having a clear goal defined during Sprint Planning.

---

### 🛒 Product Backlog

* A **prioritized list of all work items**.
* Contains:
  * Epics
  * User Stories
  * Bugs
  * Technical tasks
* Owned and prioritized by the **Product Owner**.
* The backlog evolves continuously as new requirements emerge or priorities shift. It is a living document that reflects the current understanding of the project needs.

---

### ⭕ Epic

* A **high-level business objective**.
* Too large to complete in a single sprint.
* Broken down into multiple user stories.
* Epics often represent major features or functionalities and serve as containers for related user stories.

**Example:**
Online Credit Card Application Flow

---

### 🔰 User Story (US)

* A **requirement written from the end-user’s perspective**.
* Focuses on *who*, *what*, and *why*.

**Format:**

```
As a <user>,
I want <feature>,
So that <benefit>.
```

* User stories are concise and promote conversation between team members to clarify details. They are not meant to be exhaustive specifications but rather starting points for discussion.

---

### ✅ Task

* A **small, actionable unit of work**.
* Created by the team from user stories.
* Assigned to developers or QA.
* Tasks are granular enough to be completed within a day or two, ensuring progress is visible and manageable.
