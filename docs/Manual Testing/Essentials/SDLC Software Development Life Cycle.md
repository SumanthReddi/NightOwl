## Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC)

The **Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC)** is a structured process used to design, develop, and test high-quality software.

It defines how software is **planned, built, tested, and maintained** throughout its lifecycle.

:::info Why SDLC Matters
Understanding SDLC is important for testers because it shows **where testing fits** and how it contributes to delivering a **reliable and successful product**.
:::

---

## 📖 What is SDLC?

**Definition**

SDLC is a framework that defines the steps involved in developing software, from **initial planning** to **deployment and maintenance**.

**Goal**

Ensure the software is delivered:

- On **time**
- Within **budget**
- Meeting **user and business requirements**

---

## 🔄 Phases of SDLC

Each phase of SDLC has specific **objectives and deliverables**.  
Testing contributes to almost **every phase of the lifecycle**.

---

### 1️⃣ Requirement Analysis

**What happens**

- Business requirements are gathered
- Stakeholders define what the system should do

**Role of Testing**

- Review requirements for **clarity and testability**
- Identify **missing requirements or ambiguities**

:::tip Tester Involvement
Early involvement of testers helps prevent defects **before development even begins**.
:::

---

### 2️⃣ Design Phase

**What happens**

- System architecture is created
- Technical design documents are prepared

**Role of Testing**

- Review design documents
- Identify potential **risk areas**
- Prepare **test strategy and test scenarios**

---

### 3️⃣ Implementation (Coding)

**What happens**

- Developers write the actual code based on design documents

**Role of Testing**

- Developers perform **unit testing**
- QA collaborates with developers to ensure **code quality**

---

### 4️⃣ Testing Phase

**What happens**

- Testers execute test cases
- Defects are identified and reported

**Types of testing performed**

- **System Testing**
- **Integration Testing**
- **User Acceptance Testing (UAT)**

Goal:

Ensure the software **works correctly and meets requirements**.

---

### 5️⃣ Deployment

**What happens**

- The application is released to production

**Role of Testing**

- Perform **smoke testing**
- Validate that the application works in the **production environment**

Example checks:

- Login functionality
- Database connectivity
- Critical workflows

---

### 6️⃣ Maintenance

**What happens**

- Bugs are fixed
- New features are added
- Performance improvements are implemented

**Role of Testing**

- Perform **regression testing**
- Ensure new updates **do not break existing functionality**

---

## 🏗️ SDLC Models

Different projects use different **SDLC models** depending on project requirements.

---

### 🌊 Waterfall Model

**How it works**

A sequential process where each phase is completed **before the next phase begins**.

**Testing Role**

Testing starts **after development is completed**.

**Best for**

- Small projects
- Well-defined requirements
- Stable environments

---

### ⚡ Agile Model

**How it works**

Development happens in **short iterations called sprints**.

**Testing Role**

Testing is **continuous and integrated into every sprint**.

**Best for**

- Dynamic projects
- Frequently changing requirements
- Fast development cycles

---

### 🚀 DevOps

**How it works**

Development and operations work together for **continuous delivery and deployment**.

**Testing Role**

Automation testing and CI/CD pipelines ensure **fast feedback and stable releases**.

**Best for**

- Cloud applications
- Frequent releases
- High scalability systems

---

## 🤝 Why SDLC is Important for Testers

- **Early Involvement** – Testers help review requirements and reduce risks
- **Clear Responsibilities** – Each phase defines testing tasks
- **Better Collaboration** – Improves coordination between developers, testers, and stakeholders

---

## 📌 Key Takeaways

- SDLC is a **structured framework for developing software**
- Testing plays a role in **every phase of the lifecycle**
- Common SDLC models include **Waterfall, Agile, and DevOps**
- Testers contribute to **quality assurance throughout development**
